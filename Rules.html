Billiards
Rules
- Billiards mandates the use of three balls per game: red, yellow, and white.
- Each player possesses a distinct cue ball—one with the white ball and the other with the yellow ball.
- Before commencing the game, players determine the initial breaker by simultaneously striking their cue balls down the table's length, rebounding off a cushion, and returning. The player whose cue ball comes closest to the baulk cushion following this shot earns the privilege to choose who breaks.
- The red ball is then positioned on the Billiards table, and the designated breaker places their cue ball in the D to initiate play.
- Thereafter, players take turns aiming to amass the highest score to secure victory. Points can be obtained through three methods:



❖ In-off: When your cue ball contacts one or more balls before descending into a pocket (2 / 3 points).
❖ Pot: When any ball other than your cue ball enters a pocket (2 / 3 points).
❖ Cannon: Achieved when the cue ball strikes both other balls (2 points).
- Players retain their turn at the table until failing to make a scoring shot.
- Post-foul, the opposing player retains the option to either reset all balls to their original positions or maintain the table's current state.
- The game concludes once a player reaches the predetermined winning point total.
